# FAQ and Troubleshooting Guide

This document is the entry point for operators who are new to the TraceMind platform. It collects the most frequently asked questions from new operators and the answers that the experienced team has developed over time. The questions are organized by topic and progress from basic orientation to specific troubleshooting scenarios. For deeper information on any topic, refer to the dedicated documents in the knowledge base (for example, the Retry Policy and DLT Policy documents cover those areas in much more detail).

## Orientation Questions

Q: What is TraceMind?
A: TraceMind is an event-driven platform for processing member communications. It ingests CSV files, validates and routes each record, and delivers communications through email, print, or archive. Every event in the system is logged to Splunk for observability.

Q: How do I see what is happening in the platform right now?
A: Use the MCP server's tools. The get_events_by_service tool shows event volume by service, get_events_by_stage shows event volume by stage, and get_failed_jobs shows recent failures. Together, these give you a real-time view of platform activity.

Q: What is the ProcessLog model?
A: It is the standardized format for every event log in the platform. Each event includes the jobId, recordId, service, stage, status, and timestamp. This standardization is what makes cross-service queries possible.

## Job Investigation Questions

Q: A user reports that job JOB-123 is stuck. How do I investigate?
A: Use the get_job_timeline tool with the jobId JOB-123. The tool returns all events for the job in chronological order. Look at the most recent event: if it is from a long time ago, the service that was processing the job may be hung. Check the service's health and consider restarting it.

Q: How do I find out why a specific job failed?
A: Use get_job_timeline. The first event with status FAILED is the root cause. Read its error message. The error message is generated by the service that was processing the event and typically explains exactly what went wrong.

Q: How long should a job take to process?
A: It depends on the size of the job. A small job (under 1000 records) typically completes in 5-10 minutes. A medium job (1000-10000 records) takes 15-30 minutes. A large job (over 10000 records) can take an hour or more. Use get_processing_duration to see actual processing times for recent jobs.

## Email-Specific Questions

Q: Why are emails not being delivered?
A: Use get_failed_emails to see the specific failure messages from the email-service. The most common causes are invalid email addresses in the source data, SMTP server outages, and template rendering errors. The error message will tell you which one is affecting your specific case.

Q: How do I check whether a specific member received their email?
A: Use get_record_timeline with the recordId. The timeline will show the email-service's attempt to send the email, with status SUCCESS or FAILED and the timestamp. If the status is SUCCESS, the email was accepted by the SMTP server; whether it reached the member's inbox depends on the member's email provider.

Q: What happens if the SMTP server is down for an hour?
A: The email-service will retry each failed delivery with exponential backoff. If the SMTP server is back up within the retry window (typically a few minutes), the deliveries will succeed. If the SMTP server is down for longer, some events may be routed to the DLT. These can be reprocessed once the SMTP server is back.

## Record Investigation Questions

Q: How do I trace a single record across all services?
A: Use get_record_timeline with the recordId. The tool returns every event related to that record, in chronological order, across all services. This is the most powerful debugging tool for record-level issues.

Q: A record is in the DLT. What do I do?
A: Use get_dlt_events and filter by the recordId (or by the jobId to see all DLT events for the job). Read the error message to understand the root cause. If the issue is transient (and has since been resolved), reprocess the event. If the issue is a data quality problem, fix the data and reprocess. If the record is known-bad data, document the discard decision.

Q: A record is showing in two different stages. Is that a bug?
A: Not necessarily. Some records are processed by multiple services in sequence (for example, pre-processor then email-service then archival-service). Each service emits its own ProcessLog entries, so the record will appear in events from multiple services. Use the timeline to see the sequence and confirm that it is the expected sequence.

## Operational Health Questions

Q: How do I know if the platform is healthy?
A: Check the get_events_by_service and get_events_by_stage tools. The event volume should be roughly steady (or follow the expected daily pattern). A sudden drop in event volume indicates that something is stuck. A sudden spike indicates a flood of events that may overwhelm downstream services.

Q: What is a normal retry rate?
A: Under 1% of events should require retry. A retry rate above 1% indicates stress in the system — investigate the most retried events to identify the common cause. A retry rate above 5% is a critical alert and requires immediate investigation.

Q: What is a normal DLT rate?
A: Near zero. A non-zero DLT rate means that some events are unrecoverable. The DLT backlog should be reviewed daily and kept small. A growing DLT backlog is a critical alert and requires immediate investigation.

Q: How do I know if a service is down?
A: Check get_events_by_service. If a service's event count has dropped to zero (or near zero) for a sustained period, the service may be down. Also check the platform's monitoring dashboards for service health metrics (JVM heap, thread count, Kafka consumer lag).

## Tool Selection Questions

Q: When should I use get_job_timeline vs get_record_timeline?
A: Use get_job_timeline when you have a jobId and want to see all events for the job (which may include hundreds or thousands of records). Use get_record_timeline when you have a recordId and want to see all events for that specific record (which is typically 3-10 events).

Q: When should I use get_failed_jobs vs get_failed_emails?
A: Use get_failed_jobs when you want to see all failures across all services. Use get_failed_emails when you specifically want to see email-service failures (and you want to filter out failures from other services like print-service or archival-service).

Q: When should I use get_retry_events vs get_dlt_events?
A: Use get_retry_events when you want to see events that are still being retried (and may yet succeed). Use get_dlt_events when you want to see events that have exhausted retries and require manual intervention.
